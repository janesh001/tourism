The Monument of Love

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh. It was commissioned in 1631 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is widely recognized as "the jewel of Muslim art in India" and is one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage.

History & Architecture

Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632 and was completed in 1653. It required the labor of 20,000 artisans under the guidance of a board of architects led by Ustad Ahmad Lahauri. The tomb is the central focus of the entire complex and is built on a square plinth. The entire structure is perfectly symmetrical, except for the tomb of Shah Jahan, which was added later and disrupts the perfect balance.

The Taj Mahal combines elements from Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles. The main dome is 35 meters high and is surrounded by four smaller domes. The intricate marble inlay work features semi-precious stones forming floral patterns and calligraphy.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit the Taj Mahal is during the cooler months from October to March. The monument is open from sunrise to sunset, except on Fridays when it's closed for prayers. Many visitors prefer to visit at sunrise or sunset when the changing light creates magical effects on the white marble.

UNESCO World Heritage Site

In 1983, the Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It was described as "the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage." The site attracts 7-8 million visitors annually, with around 200,000 from overseas.

Things to Do

  • Witness the stunning architecture and marble inlay work
  • Visit at sunrise or sunset for the best photographic opportunities
  • Explore the beautiful Mughal gardens
  • Visit the Taj Museum within the complex
  • View the Taj Mahal from Mehtab Bagh across the Yamuna River
  • Explore nearby attractions like Agra Fort and Fatehpur Sikri

How to Reach

By Air: Agra has its own airport (Kheria Airport) with limited connections. The nearest major airport is in Delhi (230 km away).

By Train: Agra is well connected by train. The Gatimaan Express and Shatabdi Express connect Delhi to Agra in about 2 hours.

By Road: Agra is connected to major cities via the Yamuna Expressway. It's about a 3-4 hour drive from Delhi.

Nearby Attractions

  • Agra Fort: A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this massive red sandstone fort was the main residence of Mughal emperors.
  • Fatehpur Sikri: An abandoned Mughal city, located 40 km from Agra, another UNESCO site.
  • Itmad-ud-Daulah: Often called the "Baby Taj," this beautiful tomb is considered a draft of the Taj Mahal.
  • Mehtab Bagh: A beautiful garden offering stunning views of the Taj Mahal from across the Yamuna River.

Travel Tips

  • Book tickets online in advance to avoid long queues
  • Arrive early morning or late afternoon for fewer crowds and better lighting
  • Wear comfortable shoes as there's quite a bit of walking
  • Photography is allowed, but tripods and additional lighting equipment are prohibited
  • Food, tobacco products, and large bags are not allowed inside
  • Hire a certified guide for detailed historical insights
  • Respect the monument - it's a mausoleum and a place of reverence
