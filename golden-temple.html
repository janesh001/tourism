The Holiest Sikh Shrine

The Golden Temple, officially known as Harmandir Sahib, is the most sacred Gurdwara of Sikhism. Located in Amritsar, Punjab, this stunning architectural marvel is covered in gold and surrounded by a holy water tank called Sarovar. The temple represents equality, with four doors opening to four directions, welcoming people from all walks of life.

History & Significance

The foundation of the Golden Temple was laid by Guru Arjan Dev Ji in 1581 and completed in 1604. The temple was later gold-plated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh in the 19th century. The temple houses the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism. What makes it unique is that it's built on a lower level, signifying humility, and has four entrances representing openness to all faiths.

Best Time to Visit

The Golden Temple is open 24/7 throughout the year. However, the best time to visit is from October to March when the weather is pleasant. Early morning (4-6 AM) and evening (7-9 PM) are the best times to witness the temple in its full glory. The temple looks absolutely stunning when illuminated at night.

Key Features

  • Harmandir Sahib: Main golden structure in the center of the Sarovar
  • Akal Takht: Seat of Sikh religious authority
  • Langar Hall: World's largest free kitchen serving 100,000+ people daily
  • Sarovar: Holy water tank believed to have healing properties
  • Jallianwala Bagh: Historic memorial garden nearby (5 min walk)
  • Clock Tower: Entrance tower with clock and ghanta ghar

Things to Do

  • Visit the main temple and pay respects at Guru Granth Sahib
  • Take a walk around the Sarovar (holy tank)
  • Participate in community kitchen (Langar) service
  • Attend the evening Palki Sahib ceremony
  • Visit Jallianwala Bagh memorial
  • Experience the Wagah Border ceremony (30 km away)
  • Stay at the free accommodation provided by the temple
  • Photography during different times of day

Langar - Community Kitchen

One of the most remarkable features of the Golden Temple is its Langar, the world's largest free kitchen that serves free meals to over 100,000 people daily regardless of religion, caste, gender, or status. The Langar runs 24/7 and is entirely run by volunteers (Sevadars). Visitors are welcome to participate in the preparation and serving of food as a form of Seva (selfless service).

Visitor Guidelines

  • Head Covering: Mandatory for everyone (scarves available at entrance)
  • Footwear: Must be removed before entering the complex
  • Dress Code: Dress modestly, cover legs and shoulders
  • Tobacco/Alcohol: Strictly prohibited in the complex
  • Cleanliness: Wash feet at the entrance

How to Reach

By Air: Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport is about 11 km from the Golden Temple.

By Train: Amritsar Junction is well connected to major cities. The temple is 2 km from the station.

By Road: Well connected by road with regular bus services from Delhi, Chandigarh, and other cities.

Travel Tips

  • Visit early morning or late evening to avoid crowds
  • Free accommodation available but book in advance
  • Respect the sanctity of the place - maintain silence inside
  • Don't miss the Langar experience - it's truly unique
  • Keep valuables secure while walking barefoot
  • Photography is allowed but be respectful
  • Stay hydrated, especially during summer months
  • Combine visit with Wagah Border ceremony in the evening
