The Lost City

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an ancient village in Karnataka known for its magnificent ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire. Spread across 4,100 hectares, Hampi features over 1,600 surviving monuments including temples, palaces, aquatic structures, and marketplaces. The surreal landscape of giant boulders and ancient architecture makes it a photographer's paradise.

History

Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire from 1336 to 1565 AD. At its peak, it was one of the richest and largest cities in the world. The empire fell after the Battle of Talikota in 1565, and the city was ransacked. Today, the ruins stand as a testament to the grandeur of the medieval Hindu kingdom.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Hampi is from October to February when the weather is pleasant. Summers (March-June) can be extremely hot. The Hampi Utsav festival in November is a great time to experience local culture with dance, music, and drama performances.

Top Attractions

  • Virupaksha Temple: Ancient functioning temple dedicated to Lord Shiva
  • Vittala Temple: Famous for stone chariot and musical pillars
  • Lotus Mahal: Beautiful palace with Indo-Islamic architecture
  • Elephant Stables: Grand structure that housed royal elephants
  • Hazara Rama Temple: Temple with intricate bas-relief panels
  • Queen's Bath: Ornate royal bathing complex
  • Matanga Hill: Best viewpoint for sunrise and sunset
  • Underground Shiva Temple: Half-submerged ancient temple

Things to Do

  • Explore ancient temple ruins and architecture
  • Climb Matanga Hill for panoramic views at sunrise
  • Visit Tungabhadra River and enjoy coracle rides
  • Rock climbing and bouldering activities
  • Rent bicycles to explore the vast ruins
  • Photography of stunning boulder landscapes
  • Visit Anegundi village across the river
  • Attend Hampi Utsav festival (November)

Local Cuisine

Hampi offers simple vegetarian Karnataka cuisine:

  • Bisi Bele Bath: Rice and lentil dish with vegetables
  • Masala Dosa: Crispy rice crepe with potato filling
  • Ragi Mudde: Finger millet balls
  • Akki Roti: Rice flatbread
  • Filter Coffee: South Indian style strong coffee

How to Reach

By Air: Nearest airports are Bellary (60 km) and Hubli (160 km). Bangalore airport is 350 km away.

By Train: Hospet Junction is the nearest railway station, 13 km from Hampi.

By Road: Well connected by road. Overnight buses available from Bangalore, Goa, and other cities.

Travel Tips

  • Rent a bicycle or scooter to explore - the site is vast
  • Carry water, sunscreen, and hat - it gets very hot
  • Wear comfortable walking shoes
  • Start early morning to avoid heat and crowds
  • Stay in Hampi Bazaar or Virupapur Gaddi for easy access
  • Respect the monuments - don't climb on fragile structures
  • Carry cash - ATMs limited and unreliable
  • Allow at least 2 days to explore properly
