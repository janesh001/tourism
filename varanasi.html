The Oldest Living City

Varanasi, also known as Banaras or Kashi, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Situated on the banks of the holy Ganges River, it is the spiritual capital of India and holds immense religious significance for Hindus. The city is a mesmerizing blend of ancient traditions, spirituality, culture, and vibrant life along the ghats.

Spiritual Significance

Varanasi is considered one of the seven sacred cities (Sapta Puri) in Hinduism. It is believed that dying in Varanasi and being cremated on the banks of the Ganges brings salvation (moksha) and liberation from the cycle of rebirth. The city has over 2000 temples, including the famous Kashi Vishwanath Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Varanasi is from October to March when the weather is pleasant. Avoid visiting during summer months (April-June) as temperatures can soar above 45¬∞C. The monsoon season (July-September) brings rainfall but the city maintains its spiritual charm year-round.

Top Attractions

  • Dashashwamedh Ghat: Most spectacular and famous ghat, evening Ganga Aarti ceremony
  • Assi Ghat: Southern-most ghat, popular for sunrise and yoga
  • Manikarnika Ghat: Main cremation ghat, oldest and most sacred
  • Kashi Vishwanath Temple: Most famous Shiva temple, gold-plated spire
  • Sarnath: Where Buddha gave his first sermon, major Buddhist site
  • Banaras Hindu University: Largest residential university in Asia
  • Ramnagar Fort: 18th-century fort and museum across the Ganges

Things to Do

  • Attend the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat
  • Take an early morning boat ride on the Ganges
  • Walk through the narrow lanes and explore ancient temples
  • Visit Sarnath Buddhist archaeological site
  • Experience traditional music and dance performances
  • Shop for Banarasi silk sarees and handicrafts
  • Try famous street food and lassi
  • Witness spiritual rituals and ceremonies at the ghats

Local Cuisine

Varanasi is famous for its unique street food and sweets:

  • Banarasi Paan: Traditional betel leaf preparation
  • Kachori Sabzi: Spicy fried bread with potato curry
  • Lassi: Creamy yogurt drink at famous shops
  • Tamatar Chaat: Unique tomato-based tangy snack
  • Malaiyo: Seasonal winter delicacy made with milk foam

How to Reach

By Air: Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport is about 25 km from the city center.

By Train: Varanasi Junction and Mughalsarai are major railway stations with connections to all major cities.

By Road: Well connected by road with regular bus services from nearby cities.

Travel Tips

  • Dress modestly and respect religious sentiments
  • Be prepared for crowds, especially during festivals
  • Hire a guide for better understanding of rituals and history
  • Start your day early to witness sunrise boat rides
  • Be cautious of touts and overly friendly strangers
  • Remove shoes before entering temples
  • Avoid monsoon season if you want to explore comfortably
  • Photography restrictions apply at some temples and ghats
