The Cultural Capital of Karnataka

Mysore, officially Mysuru, is known as the "City of Palaces" and is famous for its rich cultural heritage, magnificent Mysore Palace, vibrant Dussehra celebrations, and silk sarees. The city was the capital of the Kingdom of Mysore for nearly six centuries. Today, it maintains its old-world charm while being one of the cleanest and most well-planned cities in India.

History & Culture

Mysore was ruled by the Wadiyar dynasty, which contributed significantly to the city's cultural and architectural heritage. The city is known for its palaces, gardens, temples, and yoga centers. Mysore is also famous for its contributions to Indian classical music, dance (particularly Bharatanatyam), and incense sticks (agarbatti).

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Mysore is from October to February when the weather is pleasant. The Dussehra festival (September/October) is the best time to witness the grandeur of Mysore with special palace illuminations, processions, and cultural events lasting 10 days.

Top Attractions

  • Mysore Palace: Magnificent Indo-Saracenic palace, illuminated on Sundays
  • Chamundi Hills: Hilltop temple dedicated to Goddess Chamundeshwari
  • Brindavan Gardens: Musical fountain show, terrace gardens
  • St. Philomena's Church: Neo-Gothic architecture, one of Asia's tallest churches
  • Mysore Zoo: One of India's oldest and best-maintained zoos
  • Jaganmohan Palace: Art gallery with rare paintings
  • Karanji Lake: Nature park with butterfly conservatory
  • Rangacharlu Memorial Hall: Victorian architecture, library

Things to Do

  • Visit Mysore Palace - see illumination on Sundays and festival days
  • Climb 1000 steps to Chamundi Hills temple
  • Attend musical fountain show at Brindavan Gardens
  • Experience Dussehra festival celebrations (September/October)
  • Shop for Mysore silk sarees, sandalwood items, and incense
  • Visit Devaraja Market for spices and local products
  • Take day trip to nearby hill stations (Coorg, Ooty)
  • Learn yoga at one of many yoga centers
  • Try authentic Mysore Pak sweet

Local Cuisine

Mysore offers delicious Karnataka cuisine:

  • Mysore Masala Dosa: Crispy dosa with spicy red chutney
  • Mysore Pak: Famous sweet made from gram flour and ghee
  • Bisi Bele Bath: Rice, lentils, and vegetable one-pot meal
  • Ragi Mudde: Finger millet balls with sambar
  • Filter Coffee: Strong South Indian coffee

Shopping in Mysore

  • Mysore Silk: Famous silk sarees at Government Silk Weaving Factory
  • Sandalwood Products: Pure sandalwood items, soaps, oils
  • Incense Sticks: Mysore is India's incense capital
  • Rosewood Inlay: Traditional handicrafts
  • Mysore Paintings: Traditional art form with gold leaf

How to Reach

By Air: Mysore Airport has limited connectivity. Bangalore Airport (170 km) is the nearest major airport.

By Train: Mysore Junction is well connected to Bangalore, Chennai, and other major cities.

By Road: Excellent road connectivity. Just 3 hours from Bangalore on NH275.

Travel Tips

  • Visit palace early morning to avoid crowds
  • Sunday evening palace illumination is a must-see
  • Try to visit during Dussehra for grand celebrations
  • Hire a car or use local buses for city travel
  • Wear modest clothing when visiting religious sites
  • Shop at government emporiums for authentic products
  • Combine with visits to nearby Coorg or Ooty
  • Try local breakfast at old establishments like Vinayaka Mylari
