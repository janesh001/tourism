The Most Romantic City in India

Udaipur, known as the "City of Lakes," is one of the most romantic and picturesque cities in India. Founded in 1559 by Maharana Udai Singh II, this jewel of Rajasthan is famous for its azure lakes, magnificent palaces, vibrant culture, and stunning sunsets. The city's scenic beauty and royal heritage make it a popular destination for weddings and honeymoons.

History & Heritage

Udaipur was the capital of the Mewar Kingdom and has a glorious history of valor and romance. The city is home to several stunning palaces, including the famous Lake Palace (now a luxury hotel) which appears to float on Lake Pichola. The architectural grandeur reflects the artistic and cultural heritage of the Rajput era.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Udaipur is from October to March when the weather is pleasant. Winter months (November-February) are ideal for sightseeing. Summers (April-June) can be extremely hot. The monsoon season (July-September) brings moderate rainfall and makes the lakes come alive.

Top Attractions

  • City Palace: Majestic palace complex with museums, courtyards, and stunning architecture
  • Lake Pichola: Beautiful artificial lake with boat rides and sunset views
  • Lake Palace: Iconic white marble palace in middle of Lake Pichola (now luxury hotel)
  • Jag Mandir: Island palace on Lake Pichola, stunning architecture
  • Fateh Sagar Lake: Picturesque lake with Nehru Park island
  • Saheliyon Ki Bari: Garden of maidens with fountains and lotus pools
  • Monsoon Palace: Hilltop palace offering panoramic city views
  • Jagdish Temple: Beautiful Indo-Aryan temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu

Things to Do

  • Boat ride on Lake Pichola during sunset
  • Visit City Palace and explore its museums
  • Attend cultural shows at Bagore Ki Haveli
  • Take a ropeway ride to Karni Mata Temple
  • Explore local markets for handicrafts and miniature paintings
  • Photography at various palaces and lakes
  • Enjoy rooftop dining with lake views
  • Visit vintage car museum

Local Cuisine

Udaipur offers delicious Rajasthani cuisine:

  • Dal Baati Churma: Traditional Rajasthani staple
  • Gatte Ki Sabzi: Gram flour dumplings in spicy curry
  • Laal Maas: Spicy red meat curry
  • Mawa Kachori: Sweet stuffed fried bread
  • Rabri: Sweet condensed milk dessert

How to Reach

By Air: Maharana Pratap Airport is about 22 km from the city center, connected to major cities.

By Train: Udaipur City Railway Station is well connected to Delhi, Mumbai, Jaipur, and other major cities.

By Road: Well connected by road via National Highway 76. Regular bus services available.

Travel Tips

  • Book boat rides and cultural shows in advance
  • Visit during early morning or late evening for best photography
  • Wear comfortable shoes for palace exploration
  • Try rooftop restaurants overlooking the lakes
  • Bargain at local markets for handicrafts
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly at temples
  • Hire auto-rickshaws or rent bikes for local transport
  • Don't miss the sunset views from various vantage points
