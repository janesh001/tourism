God's Own Country

Kerala's backwaters are a network of brackish lagoons and lakes lying parallel to the Arabian Sea coast. This unique ecosystem spans over 900 km of interconnected waterways, rivers, lakes, and inlets. The backwaters experience is incomplete without a ride on traditional houseboats called 'Kettuvallam', offering a serene journey through palm-fringed canals and villages.

About Kerala Backwaters

The backwaters were formed by the action of waves and shore currents creating low barrier islands across the mouths of many rivers flowing down from the Western Ghats. These waterways are used for transportation, fishing, and tourism. The most popular backwater destinations include Alleppey, Kumarakom, Kollam, and Kochi.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kerala backwaters is from November to February when the weather is pleasant. The monsoon season (June-September) brings heavy rainfall but offers a unique, lush green experience. Summer months (March-May) can be hot and humid.

Top Destinations

  • Alleppey (Alappuzha): Venice of the East, most popular houseboat destination
  • Kumarakom: Luxury backwater destination on Vembanad Lake
  • Kollam: Gateway to backwaters with 8-hour cruise to Alleppey
  • Kochi (Cochin): Port city with Chinese fishing nets and spice markets
  • Kuttanad: Below sea-level farming region, unique ecosystem
  • Thiruvallam: Less crowded, authentic backwater experience

Things to Do

  • Stay in a traditional houseboat and cruise through the backwaters
  • Watch sunrise and sunset over the tranquil waters
  • Visit local villages and experience traditional Kerala lifestyle
  • Try authentic Kerala cuisine prepared onboard
  • Bird watching - spot various species of migratory and local birds
  • Experience Ayurvedic spa and wellness treatments
  • Visit spice plantations and tea gardens
  • Attend Kathakali dance performances and boat races

Local Cuisine

Kerala is famous for its delicious cuisine:

  • Kerala Sadya: Traditional vegetarian feast served on banana leaf
  • Karimeen Pollichathu: Pearl spot fish marinated and grilled
  • Appam with Stew: Rice pancakes with coconut milk stew
  • Kerala Prawn Curry: Spicy seafood delicacy
  • Puttu and Kadala: Steamed rice cake with black chickpea curry

How to Reach

By Air: Cochin International Airport (COK) and Trivandrum Airport (TRV) are the nearest airports.

By Train: Major railway stations include Ernakulam, Kottayam, Alleppey, and Kollam.

By Road: Kerala has excellent road connectivity with regular bus services from major cities.

Travel Tips

  • Book houseboats in advance, especially during peak season
  • Choose certified and licensed houseboat operators
  • Carry mosquito repellent and light cotton clothing
  • Try to avoid weekends and public holidays for better rates
  • Respect local culture and traditions
  • Stay hydrated and use sunscreen
  • Don't miss the local toddy shops for authentic experience
  • Combine backwaters with hill stations like Munnar for varied experience
