The Royal City of Rajasthan

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is known as the Pink City due to the distinctive colored buildings in the old city. Founded in 1727 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, Jaipur is a vibrant blend of ancient culture and modern lifestyle. The city is home to magnificent palaces, historic forts, vibrant bazaars, and rich cultural heritage.

History & Culture

Jaipur was India's first planned city, designed by Vidyadhar Bhattacharya according to Vastu Shastra and Shilpa Shastra. The city was painted pink in 1876 to welcome Prince Albert and Queen Victoria, and the tradition has been maintained ever since. Jaipur forms part of the famous Golden Triangle tourist circuit along with Delhi and Agra.

The city is renowned for its jewelry, textiles, handicrafts, and precious and semi-precious stones. The traditional Rajasthani culture is evident in the city's architecture, cuisine, dance, and festivals.

Top Attractions

  • Amber Fort: Magnificent hilltop fort with stunning architecture and elephant rides
  • City Palace: Royal residence with museums displaying artifacts and textiles
  • Hawa Mahal: Iconic five-story palace with 953 small windows
  • Jantar Mantar: UNESCO World Heritage astronomical observatory
  • Nahargarh Fort: Fort offering panoramic views of the city
  • Jal Mahal: Beautiful water palace in the middle of Man Sagar Lake
  • Albert Hall Museum: Oldest museum in Rajasthan with diverse collection
  • Jaigarh Fort: Fort housing the world's largest wheeled cannon

Shopping in Jaipur

Jaipur is a shopper's paradise, famous for:

  • Jewelry (precious and semi-precious stones, Kundan work)
  • Textiles (Bandhani, Block prints, Blue pottery)
  • Handicrafts (Lac bangles, Mojris, Carpets)
  • Famous markets: Johari Bazaar, Bapu Bazaar, Tripolia Bazaar

Local Cuisine

Must-try Rajasthani dishes in Jaipur:

  • Dal Baati Churma: Traditional Rajasthani staple dish
  • Laal Maas: Spicy mutton curry
  • Ghewar: Traditional sweet dessert
  • Pyaaz Kachori: Spicy fried snack
  • Lassi: Refreshing yogurt drink

How to Reach

By Air: Jaipur International Airport is well connected to major Indian cities and international destinations.

By Train: Jaipur Junction is a major railway station connected to all major cities in India.

By Road: Jaipur is connected via National Highway 8 to Delhi (280 km) and Agra (240 km).

Travel Tips

  • Wear comfortable shoes for exploring forts and palaces
  • Bargain in local markets - it's part of the culture
  • Try to visit major attractions early morning to avoid crowds
  • Hire a certified guide for better understanding of history
  • Stay hydrated, especially during summer months
  • Try local street food but choose clean establishments
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly at religious sites
  • Book elephant rides at Amber Fort in advance
